#define

#define is an intercollegiate hackathon which aims to challenge the members of the academic community to come up with novel software solutions to existing problems. Participants must develop an application following the central theme within 24 hours and present it to a panel.

The Challenge

Lack of clean water.
Mining accidents.
Natural Disasters.
Misuse of resources.
Depletion of the ozone layer.
Vulnerabilities in cyberspace security.


Big or small, these are some of the problems faced by the current generation. Engineers and scientists do everything they can to address issues such as these.

Truly, the rate of how fast scientific advances are made and technological breakthroughs are created is both astounding and overwhelming. Every day is a step in making the world a better home for the future generations with every one everywhere trying to make a contribution.

What can we, programmers, software engineers, and developers, do?

This is the grand challenge of #define (hashdefine).

Innovation, redefined.

Frequently Asked Questions

(1) Is there a registration fee?

Registration is absolutely free!

(2) What are the requirements to register?

Undergraduate students from any university may form teams of three (3) to five (5) members each. All of the team members must be officially enrolled for the current semester.

(3) Are we allowed to substitute members?

Substitutions will only be allowed if they advise the organizers at least twelve (12) hours before the start of the event.

(4) When is the deadline of registration?

All registration requirements must be submitted by 22 January 2016, 11:59 PM PST. Teams will be notified by e-mail regarding the status of their registration within 48 hours.

(5) Will we be automatically in upon registration?

Unfortunately, we can only accommodate the first thirty (30) teams to ensure that the participants get the best experience possible.

(6) Are we allowed to use external hardware devices (such as Raspberry Pi, other examples here)?

The use of hardware devices is not allowed.

(7) Are we restricted to any API’s?

You may use any available API’s given that you have the proper rights and permissions to do so.

(8) Do we have to stay in the building the entire time?

For security and safety considerations, you may opt to go out before the “lockout period” but you may not return until that period is up.

(9) Will our creation be our own intellectual property or will it belong to another entity?

All submissions remain the intellectual property of the participants or teams that conceptualized and developed them.

(10) Are there prizes?

You can win as much as PhP 25,000. There will also be freebies during the event proper.

(11) Is there a pitching portion?

Yes, each team may send a maximum of two members. They will be given at most seven (7) minutes for the pitching portion.

(12) Will our code be judged?

Yes. It will be reviewed by a panel dedicated to code-review.

For additional questions, you may email hashdefine.hacks@gmail.com!
